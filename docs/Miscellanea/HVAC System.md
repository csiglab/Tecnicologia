## Heat Removal Methods

| **Method** | **Principle** | **Used In** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Vapor Compression Cycle** | Uses a refrigerant in a closed loop; heat is absorbed by **evaporation** at low pressure and released during **condensation** at high pressure. | Most residential and commercial AC systems |
| **Evaporative Cooling** | Heat is removed by **evaporation of water**, which absorbs latent heat from air. | Swamp coolers, arid climate coolers |
| **Absorption Cooling** | Uses a **thermal energy source** (e.g., gas, solar) instead of electricity to drive the refrigeration cycle using a refrigerantâ€“absorbent pair (e.g., waterâ€“LiBr). | Industrial cooling, cogeneration |
| **Thermal Storage Cooling** | Removes heat by **shifting cooling load** to off-peak times using stored chilled water or ice. | District cooling, energy-efficient systems |
| **Heat Exchange with Outside Air** | Uses **ventilation and air exchange** with outdoor air when outside is cooler. | Economizer mode, passive systems |
| **Geothermal Heat Pumps** | Transfers heat to/from **underground thermal mass** using fluid loops. | Ground-source HVAC systems |
| **Thermoelectric Cooling** | Uses the **Peltier effect** to create a temperature difference via solid-state electronics. | Compact or portable cooling systems |
| **Radiative Cooling** | Surfaces emit infrared radiation to **cool below ambient**, especially at night. | Emerging tech in passive cooling |
